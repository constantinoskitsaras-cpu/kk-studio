# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary: recruiters and studios evaluating Konstantinos Kitsaras for hire or collaboration.** VFX / game / automotive-visualization studios and creative leads who land on the site to judge craft, range, and tooling fast, then decide whether to reach out. They scan for proof before they read prose.

**Secondary: automotive brands and agencies** looking for someone to take on a CGI commission or campaign — brand/marketing managers and producers sizing up fit and seriousness.

Both arrive with the same underlying question — *is this person good enough, and can I work with them?* — and both should leave with a strong impression of the work **and** a low-friction way to start a conversation.

## Product Purpose

A one-person automotive CGI studio portfolio. It exists to make a recruiter or client believe in the work within seconds and act on it — view the projects, then send an inquiry. Success is a qualified inbound message (hire or commission) from someone who already trusts the craft because the site demonstrated it.

The site spans home, work (index + project detail), about, contact, and a dormant store (limited studio merch, "coming soon").

**Strategic tension to hold:** the live copy currently positions the studio for *commissions* ("brands, agencies, productions"), but the #1 visitor is a studio/recruiter evaluating for *hire*. Copy and hierarchy should speak to both readings without collapsing into either — show employable craft and tooling depth (for recruiters) while staying commission-ready (for brands).

## Brand Personality

**Cinematic · Precision · Confident.**

Voice: assured, specific, never salesy. Short declarative lines over marketing adjectives ("I make automotive CGI that looks shot, not rendered" — not "stunning premium visuals"). The work carries the weight; the words get out of its way. Dark, deliberate, controlled. Emotional goal: the visitor thinks *"how was this made?"* and *"I want this person on the project."*

## Anti-references

- **Cheap / hobbyist.** Nothing that reads amateur or budget. This is the hardest fail to avoid and the most important — premium finish in every detail.
- **Generic SaaS / template landing.** No card grids, no tiny tracked eyebrows above every section, no hero-metric template, no startup-deck scaffolding.
- **ArtStation-style thumbnail grid.** The work must be presented with identity and curation — large, deliberate frames and a point of view — not an undifferentiated gallery wall of thumbnails.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the argument.** Show, don't tell. Imagery and real project frames lead; copy supports. A recruiter should grasp craft before reading a sentence. (Implication: zero-image placeholders are a bug — real renders must land here.)
2. **Premium through restraint.** One deliberate accent (lime), disciplined motion, generous space. Restraint reads as confidence — but never as emptiness or "safe." Distinctive + refined, not timid.
3. **Built for fast evaluation.** The primary visitor is judging in seconds. Clear hierarchy, scannable project range, visible tooling/role per project. Make "is this good + can I work with them" answerable above the fold and one click deep.
4. **Cinematic precision.** Every frame, transition, and interaction is deliberate and earns its place. Nothing decorative; motion guides attention, it doesn't perform.
5. **Escape the category reflex.** Actively differentiate from the ArtStation-grid and SaaS-template defaults — and stay self-aware about the saturated dark-editorial-serif lane the site currently sits in. Identity carries the page, not trend.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1, large/bold text ≥3:1 against the dark surface (watch the muted `#3D3D3D` and `#7A7A7A` grays — verify each against `#090909`/`#111111`). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support with meaningful non-motion fallbacks (the site already honors it; keep hover/reveal effects degrading gracefully). Keyboard-navigable, visible focus states (lime focus ring is in place), and descriptive alt text on all project imagery as part of the brand voice.
